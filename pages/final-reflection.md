# Final Reflection

Over the semester, my understanding of artificial intelligence changed significantly. At the beginning of the course, I mostly thought about AI as a useful productivity tool. Through the projects and readings in DH101, I began to understand AI as a complex system connected to labor, infrastructure, environmental cost, and human decision-making.

One of the most important ideas I explored throughout the semester was that AI systems are not abstract or fully autonomous. Projects such as the AI infrastructure map and network visualization revealed the global systems of extraction, manufacturing, and labor that support AI technologies. These projects helped me understand how power and inequality are distributed through technological systems.

Another major theme was authorship and creativity. In projects such as the AIDA chatbot and Prompt Engineer game, I explored how creativity changes when working with generative systems. Rather than directly producing every output, creative work increasingly involves designing prompts, systems, and structures while curating and interpreting results.

The course also changed how I think about ethics and visibility. Projects focused on content moderation labor and AI ecology demonstrated that many of the costs of AI are intentionally hidden from users. Labor systems, environmental impacts, and infrastructure are often invisible despite being essential to how AI functions.

Overall, this course taught me that being human in the age of AI means remaining critically aware of the systems behind technology while still taking responsibility for how we use and interpret these tools.
