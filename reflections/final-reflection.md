# Final Reflection

Over the semester, my understanding of AI changed significantly. At the beginning of the course, I mostly viewed AI as a tool for efficiency and automation. Through the projects and readings in DH 101, I began to understand AI as a system that reshapes creativity, labor, authorship, and human decision-making.

One major theme throughout the course was that AI systems are not neutral. In projects such as the network visualization, I explored the hidden labor and material infrastructure behind AI systems. This showed me that AI depends on global supply chains, extraction, and human labor that are often invisible to users.

Another important idea was that creativity changes in generative systems. In my chatbot and game projects, I realized that human creativity increasingly involves designing systems, prompts, and structures rather than directly producing every output. AI can generate endless possibilities, but humans still decide what matters through interpretation and curation.

The course also made me think critically about authorship and agency. AI systems can imitate creativity, but they do not possess intention or understanding. Human judgment remains essential in giving meaning to generated outputs.

Overall, this course taught me that being human in the age of AI means remaining critically aware of how these systems influence creativity, labor, and decision-making while still taking responsibility for how we use them.
