# Week 11 – AI & Labor


## Artifact

This project explored the environmental costs of artificial intelligence, including energy use, carbon emissions, and large-scale AI infrastructure.

## Reflection

This project focused on the environmental impact of AI systems, especially the carbon emissions associated with large-scale model training and everyday AI usage.

One of the most surprising discoveries was that training a model such as GPT-3 could produce hundreds of metric tons of CO₂ emissions. Even individual prompts contribute to energy consumption when scaled globally.

Before this project, I rarely thought about AI as a physical infrastructure system. AI felt invisible and abstract. However, researching data centers, energy consumption, and emissions made the environmental costs much more tangible.

I also learned that many companies are not fully transparent about AI-related emissions, making it difficult to measure exact impacts.

This project argues that sustainability is not only a technological issue, but also a political and ethical issue involving corporate responsibility, energy systems, and public awareness.

The project made me more conscious about how often and why I use AI systems in everyday life.
