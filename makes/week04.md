# Week 4 — GIF Remix

## Artifact

This project explored how looping motion changes the meaning of images through a water polo GIF animation.

## Reflection

Creating a looping GIF changed how I thought about images and motion. A still image captures a single moment, but when an image repeats, it creates a sense of rhythm and ongoing action.

In my GIF, the water polo ball moves back and forth between two players in a continuous loop. Instead of showing one pass, the repetition turns the moment into a pattern of play. The loop makes the action feel endless, reflecting how sports involve repeated movement and positioning.

Looping also changes how viewers pay attention to the image. Because the action repeats, viewers begin noticing small details they might miss in a still image. The repetition creates a playful and slightly hypnotic effect.

This project also complicated ideas of authorship because the final result came from both human decisions and automated processes. While the software generated and repeated the frames automatically, I controlled the concept, pacing, and structure of the animation.

## AI Use

- Tools Used: Canva, ChatGPT
- Human Contribution: Theme selection, motion design, interpretation, and editing
