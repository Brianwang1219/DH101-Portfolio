# Week 9 – Bots & Generators

## Artifact

This Twine game explored authorship, creativity, and control in human-AI collaboration.

## Reflection

In this project, players take on the role of a “prompt engineer” attempting to guide an AI system through different prompting strategies.

The game explored how input structure shapes AI-generated output. Players could choose to be direct, creative, or vague, each producing different outcomes. This reflected real-world interactions with generative AI systems.

The most important idea in the project was that AI does not independently create meaning. Instead, meaning emerges through interaction between human prompts and AI responses.

The game also explored procedural rhetoric. Rather than directly arguing about AI authorship, the game demonstrated these ideas through player choices and outcomes.

One of the key tensions in the game was that even when players refined prompts carefully, the AI output still felt partially outside their control. This complicated traditional ideas of ownership and authorship.

This project helped me understand games as systems that communicate arguments through interaction and rules rather than only through narrative.

## AI Use

- Tools Used: Twine, ChatGPT (brainstorming and wording support)
- Human Contribution: Narrative structure, branching choices, writing, editing, and game design
