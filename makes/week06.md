
# Week 6 — Digital Map of AI Infrastructure

## Artifact

This project mapped the global infrastructure behind artificial intelligence, including data centers, mining regions, corporate headquarters, and outsourced labor hubs.

## Reflection

This project challenged the idea that AI is purely digital or cloud-based. Through mapping, I discovered that AI depends on highly material systems involving extraction, labor, energy, and global logistics.

One of the most difficult parts of the project was researching invisible labor systems such as content moderation and data labeling. Unlike corporate headquarters or data centers, these labor systems are intentionally less visible.

The map revealed strong geographic inequalities. Corporate decision-making power is concentrated in the United States and other wealthy countries, while extraction and labor are often outsourced to the Global South.

This project showed that mapping is not neutral. The process of selecting layers and relationships creates an argument about power, visibility, and inequality.

The project was heavily influenced by ideas from *Atlas of AI* and Data Feminism, particularly the importance of making invisible systems visible.

## AI Use
